Painting

Urban Dreams

Created in 2022 Acrylic on Canvas 48" × 36"
Back to Portfolio

Urban Dreams represents my exploration of the vibrant, chaotic energy that pulses through modern cities. This piece was born from countless early morning walks through downtown streets, where I observed how light dances across glass facades and shadows create unexpected patterns on concrete.

The color palette—dominated by electric blues, warm oranges, and deep purples—captures the artificial glow of city nights blending with natural dawn light. Each brushstroke was intentionally bold and gestural, mimicking the constant movement and urgency of urban life.

The Creative Process

I began this piece with no preliminary sketches, allowing the painting to evolve organically. Using large brushes and palette knives, I built up layers of acrylic paint, sometimes waiting for sections to dry, other times working wet-on-wet to create unexpected color interactions.

Inspiration & Meaning

The title "Urban Dreams" reflects the aspirations and possibilities that cities represent—places where millions of individual dreams converge and collide. The abstract forms suggest buildings, roads, and infrastructure, but remain ambiguous enough to invite personal interpretation.

I wanted viewers to feel the dual nature of urban spaces: the excitement and opportunity they offer, alongside the overwhelming sensory overload they can create. The painting intentionally lacks a clear focal point, encouraging eyes to wander across the canvas much like walking through a bustling city street.

Technical Details

Artwork Specifications

Medium: Acrylic on Canvas
Dimensions: 48" × 36" × 1.5"
Year: 2022
Frame: Gallery wrapped, ready to hang
Signature: Signed on back with certificate of authenticity

Exhibition History

Urban Dreams was first exhibited at the Contemporary Visions gallery show in spring 2022, where it received positive attention from collectors and critics alike. It has since been featured in several group exhibitions exploring themes of modern life and urbanization.
